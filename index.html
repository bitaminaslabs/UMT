Alginate: Ion Exchange Mechanism

Sorbsan & SequaSorb

When applied to a wound, the sodium alginate fibers absorb wound fluid. A critical ionic exchange occurs:

Calcium ions (Ca++) are released into the wound bed.

Sodium ions (Na+) from the exudate are drawn into the fiber.

Result: The dressing transforms into a soft, hydrophilic gel.

SequaCel: Carboxymethyl Cellulose

Vertical Wicking Action

SequaCel absorbs exudate strictly in a vertical direction. This locks fluid inside the dressing fibers rather than allowing lateral spread, preventing peri-wound maceration.

Structural Integrity

Composed of a sterile soft nonwoven pad or ribbon that remains intact even when fully saturated, facilitating easy one-piece removal.

Chitosan & Antimicrobial Variants

Chitosan (Kytocel & SequaSan)

Derived from 100% natural chitosan fibres, these highly conformable dressings are designed to manage moderate to highly exuding wounds.

  • Facilitates autolytic debridement of necrotic tissue.
  • Coherent gel formation allows for intact, pain-free removal.
  • Excellent wet-strength integrity.

Silver (Ag+) Formulations

Available in formulations like Sorbsan Silver and SequaSorb Ag+.

  • Proactive antimicrobial management.
